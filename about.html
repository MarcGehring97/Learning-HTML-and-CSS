[work in progress] Good thoughts are precious. Our societies have beyond their dependency on physical labor. This gives us seemingly unlimited opportunities to use our minds to shape our world according to our liking. While I strongly identify with this thought, I am also aware of its limitations. This very idea can lead to such shortsighted behavior as exhibited by numerous governments globally. I’m talking about ignoring climate change and social inequality issues. Public indifference in these matters is detrimental to […] Science inspires me and I am willing to dedicate myself to the advance of our societies (our global society) by applying it, but I will not tune out political abuse for power and reputation. We all have to take a firm stand against any kind of racism, sexism, and any other form of prejudicial or discriminating attitude against any other human being. Built on a solid foundation of sustainable and social societies, then, we will be able to solve any challenge that comes our way. As one race, we have accomplished so much thus far but there is yet much to be explored and improved! […] In making these assertions I do not demand you to start acting entirely altruistically; I am simply of the conviction that exerting effort for the sake of supporting society effectively is equivalent to a rational, selfish approach. This idea may displease you, but please bear with me. One of the many benefits of being organized as a society is that of cooperation surplus. When we stop fighting among ourselves for our short-term personal gains, we can concentrate on improving our living conditions. This reasoning is well established in social science, particularly in economics, which I studied. Now, to this end, I say that we all need to give our best effort in any area of life. Be it studying, understanding other human beings, being well informed and checking facts, or caring about our environment, we should always at least try wholeheartedly. The attempt is good in and on itself. So, from this perspective there is a straight-forward rationale that combined self-interest with societal benefit. A government plays the following role in this paradigm: Governments, to my mind, ought to organize public goods and services that are to the benefit of multiple citizens at once, beyond their purpose of providing stability and protecting rights. Importantly, I say that the government should ideally be the only source of social services, since social services as per definition are public services. By saying “only”, I mean to exclude companies. The chief purpose of any company should be to improve itself by becoming more efficient and providing goods and services that people actually need. (Related to this, I disapprove of developments in social media and television, but this is a different topic.) Accordingly, I take a critical look social and environmental company initiatives that serve the purpose of gaining a competitive advantage (“greenwashing”). Such aims should be ignited by intrinsic motivation and stem from the awareness that social services are good. […] equity in opportunity [...] I am aware that some of my ideas are idealistic, if not utterly unrealistic or even bad. By no means do I want to be narrow-minded or too drawn to one paradigm.
